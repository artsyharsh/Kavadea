Kavadea Instagram Marketing Strategy

◈ Kavadea — Instagram Strategy
A complete mythological playbook for the world's most visual platform
01 · philosophy
Instagram as a Kavad Box
Each scroll opens another panel. Each post reveals another forgotten world. Instagram is not a sales channel — it is Kavadea's living mythology altar. Every pixel must feel ancient and futuristic simultaneously.
Tone
Poetic. Cinematic. Mysterious. Never startup-y, never generic luxury, never spiritual-guru vibes.
Aesthetic
Dark rituals meet modern street. Raw texture. Imperfect craft. The tension between ancient and futuristic.
Audience
23–35, India + NRI globally. Designers, creatives, artists, rebels, seekers. People who wear identity, not just clothing.
Goal
Tribe formation, not follower count. Cult loyalty over mass approval. Obsession over cheap virality.
02 · posting rhythm
Frequency
Kavadea must be consistent but never noisy. Quality of myth over quantity of posts.
Feed Posts
4–5 per week. Every image must earn its place in the altar.
Reels
2–3 per week. Cinematic, atmospheric. Not trend-chasing.
Stories
Daily. 5–8 frames. The living ritual space.
Ads
2–3 active campaigns. Always tied to a collection or drop.
03 · content pillars
Six pillars, one universe
Every piece of content belongs to one of these six mythological pillars. Never post anything that doesn't fit.
◈ The Mythology Drop
Introduce forgotten Indian stories, symbols, rituals, and archetypes. Not history lessons — emotional transmissions. "This symbol was used by tribes in Rajasthan to ward off spiritual hunger. We stitched it into 9 shirts."
40% of feed
◈ Product as Portal
Show the garment as a ritual object, not a product. No flat-lays. No white background e-commerce shots. The piece must feel like it carries weight — story, emotion, memory. Show it in motion, in light, on skin, in context.
25% of feed
◈ The Making (Process)
Artisan hands. Raw embroidery in progress. Kavad box art being painted. The imperfect stitch. This is proof of soul. Kavadea's craftsmanship is philosophy — show it as a ritual act, not a factory floor.
15% of feed
◈ The Tribe
Real people wearing Kavadea in their world. Not models. Creatives, artists, rebels, seekers. Shot documentary-style. Tag their identity — designer, musician, architect. Build the community altar publicly.
10% of feed
◈ Desire & Obsession
Abstract, atmospheric, psychological content. A poem about longing. A reel of a symbol dissolving into smoke. A quote from a forgotten oral tradition. Content that makes people feel something they can't name — and come back for more.
7% of feed
◈ Behind the Codex
The mind behind the mythology. Why a collection was born. What emotion demanded expression. The research into forgotten lore. Show the brand's intellectual depth and creative obsession without being academic.
3% of feed
04 · feed posts
What to post
Every post is a panel opening in the Kavad box. Visual rules: dark moody palettes, raw textures, film grain, natural light or ritual candlelight. Never bright commercial photography.
Collection editorial
Multi-image editorial shoot per collection
6–10 slide carousel. Each slide is a panel of the story. Start with the emotion, end with the piece. Caption reads like a myth, not a product description. Example: "Thread Narratives — 9 shirts. 9 memories. Each stitch placed by a hand that has stitched for 30 years."
Symbol post
One symbol. One story. One garment.
Single image of a specific motif/symbol used in a piece, with a caption explaining its forgotten origin. Drives deep cultural credibility. Example: Image of a hand-painted kavad motif — caption reveals the story the Kavad storyteller would have told with that panel.
Artisan portrait
Portrait of the maker, story of the hand
Close portrait of the artisan with their work. Name, village, craft, years of practice. "Ramji. 43 years. Jaisalmer. His hands remember patterns his grandfather drew in sand." Builds emotional depth, not craft-brand nostalgia.
Desire quote
Typographic mythology card
A powerful line from brand language or from forgotten oral lore, rendered in Kavadea's typographic style. Dark background, raw texture, single poetic line. "The symbol you don't understand — that one is yours." Shareable, re-saveable.
Tribe feature
Community altar: who wears Kavadea
Photo of a real buyer/community member in their world. Shot in their space — studio, street, studio rooftop. Their words about why they chose the piece. "Mihir. Architect. Mumbai. He wore Object of Desire to a site he was building. Said it felt like armour."
05 · reels
Cinematic, not viral
Reels are rituals, not trends. Never use trending audio just for reach. Use original sounds, Sanskrit chants, folk instruments, ambient soundscapes, or silence. Every reel should feel like a short film fragment.
Ritual unboxing
The unboxing is a ritual, not a haul
Slow, atmospheric unboxing. Hands opening the package. The tissue. The piece emerging. Folk music or silence. Final shot: the piece laid out like a sacred object. 30–45 seconds. No voiceover. Text overlays only.
Making process
From feeling to fabric — the creation reel
Timelapse + slow motion hybrid. Raw material → artisan hand → finished garment. End on the garment being worn in a sacred or charged environment. "This piece took 18 days. One emotion. One myth. One pair of hands."
Mythology short
60-second forgotten story
Visual poetry reel telling a forgotten Indian myth/legend/ritual in 60 seconds. Text overlaid on dark visuals, folk audio. End with the garment that was born from that story. Not educational — emotional. Like a campfire story.
Lookbook
Collection lookbook reel
15–30 second atmospheric film of a collection. Shot on location — ruins, ghats, forests, rooftops. No model smiling at camera. Models in character, in ritual, in motion. Kavadea world, not fashion show.

07 · stories
Daily rituals in 24 hours
Stories are where the brand breathes in real time. They should feel intimate, exploratory, and alive — not polished advertising. Think: behind the veil.
Morning ritual
One mythology fact, symbol, or ancient story. 3–4 text slides. Poetic. No hashtags. "Today's forgotten energy:" format builds habit and return.
Raw making
Behind-the-scenes of production. Raw, unfiltered. Artisan at work. Fabric arriving. A mistake that became a feature. The texture of craft.
Question the tribe
Poll: "Which forgotten emotion do you wear: longing or obsession?" Slider: "How deep is your mythology knowledge?" This feeds the tribe's identity in public.
Drop countdown
3-day ritual countdown before any new piece drops. Each day: one teaser frame — a symbol, a material, a shadow of the garment. No price. No product shot. Just anticipation.
Tribe reposts
Reshare customer stories with captions that add mythological context to what they posted. Not a generic repost — a ritual elevation. "Arjun wears Object of Desire to every pitch. He calls it his armour. We believe him."
Sacred locations
Visit ruins, crafts markets, folk festivals, art villages. Document raw. "We went to the source." This is brand travel content with mythological purpose, not tourism.
Story of the week
Every Friday: a longer mythology story in 8–10 slides. Rituals, folk heroes, forgotten cults, regional oral traditions. Builds the most loyal, returning audience.
Link stories
When driving to shop or a new drop, the story must earn the swipe-up. Don't just show the product — tell one more layer of the story, then reveal the link.
08 · paid ads
Ads that feel like mythology, not marketing
Kavadea ads must never look like ads. They must feel like a fragment of a larger world the viewer is only now discovering. Never use "Buy Now" as primary copy. Use "Enter" / "Claim yours" / "The drop is live".
◈ Collection launch — awareness reel ad
Awareness Reels Interest targeting
Objective: Reach new audiences who feel culturally disconnected from mainstream fashion.
Format: 15–30 sec cinematic reel. No talking head. Pure visuals + text overlay + folk/ambient audio.
Hook: "Some stories were never meant to be remembered. We wore them anyway."
CTA: "Enter the collection →"
Targeting: 23–38, Bangalore/Mumbai/Delhi/Jaipur/Chandigarh + NRI diaspora in UK, US, UAE. Interests: Indian art, contemporary fashion, mythology, independent design, Auralee, Our Legacy.
◈ Desire campaign — retargeting story ad
Retargeting Stories Warm audience
Objective: Convert profile visitors and reel watchers who haven't purchased.
Format: Full-screen story. 3 slides. Slow. Text-heavy.
Slide 1: "You came back."
Slide 2: "Something here felt like yours. You weren't wrong."
Slide 3: Product shot with "Claim it before someone else does."
Targeting: Profile visitors, reel viewers (75%+), Instagram engagers — last 90 days.
◈ Artisan story — brand equity carousel ad
Brand equity Feed carousel Cold + warm
Objective: Build emotional brand depth, not just product awareness. Reduce cost-per-engagement.
Format: 5-slide carousel. Artisan portrait + story + garment reveal.
Copy: "18 days. One forgotten story. One pair of hands. One shirt that will outlive the trend cycle."
CTA: "Meet the maker →"
Note: This ad builds trust more than it drives immediate sales — run it alongside conversion campaigns.
◈ Limited drop — urgency ad
Conversion Feed + stories Warm audience
Objective: Drive sales for limited-edition drops (Thread Narratives — 9 shirts only).
Copy: "9 shirts. 9 stories. When they're gone, they're gone forever."
Visual: Flat lay of all 9 pieces arranged like offerings on an altar.
CTA: "Enter the drop"
Urgency lever: Scarcity is built into Kavadea's DNA — limited runs, hand-made quantities. Use this honestly, never artificially.

11 · repost & collaboration
Who gets a seat at the altar
Kavadea does not repost randomly. Every repost is a curation act — a statement of what the brand's world contains. This is tribe-building, not engagement farming.
Community reposts
Customers who post organically wearing Kavadea. Reshare to Stories, never to feed unless the image quality and vibe match brand aesthetic. Add mythology context to every reshare caption.
Artist collaborations
Partner with illustrators, muralists, sculptors, and folk artists who work with Indian mythology or symbolism. Their process + Kavadea piece = cross-posted content. Not influencer. Creator-as-equal.
Culture institutions
Tag and acknowledge folk art festivals, craft fairs, independent Indian design platforms (like Qyuki, Jaypore community, NIFT collectives). Be part of a cultural ecosystem.
Photographer collabs
Commission photographers who shoot documentary/editorial — not fashion photographers who use ring lights and white walls. Shoot in situ: Jodhpur streets, Banaras ghats, Bengaluru rooftops.
12 · who NOT to collaborate with
✗ Lifestyle influencers with no cultural depth
✗ Fashion haul creators / "try on" formats
✗ Bollywood celebrities (unless they are genuinely in the tribe)
✗ Brands that are "ethnic" or "sustainable" for marketing, not philosophy
✗ Anyone whose aesthetic is generic, clean, or trend-chasing
✗ Paid reviews that read like paid reviews
13 · ideal collaborators
✓ Indie musicians working with folk traditions (Prateek Kuhad adjacent, not Dua Lipa adjacent)
✓ Visual artists whose work references Indian symbolism or psychology
✓ Architects, designers, filmmakers who operate with cultural intentionality
✓ Anthropologists, writers, poets who engage with Indian oral traditions
✓ International curators and buyers who seek "mythology house" positioning

Reels: 6–8 PM IST Tuesday–Thursday for maximum reach
Stories: 7 AM (morning), 1 PM (lunch), 9 PM (night) — 3 pulses daily
15 · metrics
What Kavadea measures
Kavadea is a cult, not a mass brand. Measure depth of engagement, not width. A save matters more than a like. A DM matters more than a follow.
Saves
Most important metric. A save = "this belongs to me."
Shares
Stories shared = tribe spreading the mythology.
DMs
Unsolicited DMs = genuine emotional resonance.
Reel views 75%+
Completion rate = the film held them.
Secondary metrics (still important)
Story replies
People answering polls or replying to myth fragments = active tribe members, not passive followers.
Profile visits to bio link
Funnel quality. Are reels converting curious strangers to interested buyers?
Comment depth
Long comments > many comments. "This feels like something I've always known" = brand working.
Drop sell-through speed
How fast do limited drops sell when announced on Instagram? This is the ultimate Instagram ROI measure.
The one metric that matters most
When someone DMs: "I don't know why, but I feel like this was made for me" — that is Kavadea working perfectly. Track that sentiment. It is the brand's North Star signal on Instagram.
